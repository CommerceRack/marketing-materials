What is CommerceRack?

CommerceRack uses the opensource anyCommerce framework to create apps using javascript, html5, css3 targeted at browser, tablet, mobile. The program can be bundled and re-sold in either a success driven (fee per transaction) or flat monthly pricing model along with other cloud services.

anyCommerce: App Framework

The anyCommerce Mobile App Framework provides a superior mobile optimized, legacy website compatible, b2c + b2b in single tenant or multi-tenant form at a faster speed, and more competitive price point.

anyCommerce will be marketed as an open alternative to traditional data-siloed SAAS-based e-commerce, m-commerce, point of sale and marketplace management companies. anyCommerce software is free, but it requires a cloud platform (called a data-fair) to connect to.

anyCommerce is the best solution when old labels such as “e-commerce” and “m-commerce” get blurry with in-store, mobile, self-checkout, show-rooming, kiosk, social, marketplace are needed

DataFair: Connect It All

DataFair is an open repository for commerce data that is accessed by Apps. The DataFair provides connectivity for all retail points and is ideal for mixed-mode commerce (online and brick & mortar). Using open source HTML5, CSS, and Javascript to offer a open source commerce solution.

The DataFair model is superior for smb cloud based data management and is ideally suited to simplify many cost centers associated with retail for businesses as they transition to the cloud. CommerceRack provides a data-fair platform that can be licensed and deployed into a cloud infrastructure for resale as part of a comprehensive bundled service offering.

Apps: Versatility

Apps will be a point of differentiation: We believe in a future where a company's app (and functionality) is as important as any other aspect of its business since that is how a majority of their clients will interact with them. We include lots of sample apps - shopping apps (e-commerce web), administrative apps, point of sale apps, along with more exotic apps like call center agent apps, and checkout intercepts to seed this and show retailers the way. Rich apps (or native apps) connect to "Internet of Things" -- cameras, location, push notifications, etc

Keeping Up with Change

Companies need to keep one foot in the old, and one foot in the new.

We believe ultimately that delivering software and device capabilities will be a core component of any company (not just Internet) -- this includes everything between haircuts and waiting for a table, to purchasing an item and having it shipped, or in-store pickup. Ex: QR/NFC tags for wifi password, reserving a table (tracking progress), and loyalty point tracking to name just a few.

The current m+e+f commerce + in-store paradigm will be unified, and then all sales/digital non-digital will be broken into two categories:

  • Direct: Consumers purchase directly from retailer
  • InDirect: Consumers purchasing products and/or services through third party marketplace such as Amazon, eBay, Groupon, etc.
  • Example: Walmart will need to stop treating in-store sales as simply “in-store” when more than 50% of their clients manage their shopping list via the wal mart app and use in-store mobile checkout.

Companies who employ these strategies earlier have the opportunity to not only streamline operations, but also gain significant market share with affluent mobile customers.
